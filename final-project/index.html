My Reason

“Is human art still wanted with the new rise of ai art?” This topic is important to me as I too am an artist, though my art skills are none of noteworthy proportions I still feel obliged to talk about this. With the rise of ai art a lot of artists, including myself, have a lot of varying opinions on it. For one lots of artists are worried about how they might be replaced by ai, I have seen some really good ai art from strolling on twitter or Instagram. And I can see why they would be worried, some of the art that I’ve seen could barely be told apart if it was made by ai or a human. The only way was if it was stated or the very little mistakes from the art. It has gotten to the point that ai can copy someone else's art style, which caused a lot of problems. I’m not worried about my art style being copied, as it’s not noteworthy, but I am worried about other artists’ styles being copied. I do understand how others feel when their things get taken and credited for someone else's work, which has happened to me before, so I know the feeling of it. Which leads me to want to pursue this, as a lot of audiences either likes ai art or doesn’t. There are also a lot of people who are in the middle, people who do like the ai art often defend it even when it copies other artist's styles. Often saying that it’s just an opportunity to see other art styles in a more unique way now, and that they won't have to wait now for them to draw. Which makes me think, what do people look for when they see art? Do they just like the thing they’re looking at, or do they like the passion that artist brings in the image? If so, do they care for human artists anymore?
